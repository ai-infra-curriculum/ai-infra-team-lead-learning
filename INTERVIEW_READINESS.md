# Interview Readiness Guide – AI Infrastructure Team Lead

## Purpose
Use this guide to showcase leadership, coaching, and delivery management skills drawn from the team lead repository.

## Core Competencies to Highlight
- Building high-performing infra teams and coaching engineers  
- Balancing delivery commitments with reliability and well-being  
- Facilitating cross-functional alignment and stakeholder management  
- Creating repeatable processes (runbooks, retrospectives, hiring loops)

## Preparation Checklist
1. Select stories highlighting coaching wins, process improvements, and delivery outcomes.  
2. Collect artefacts: one-on-one frameworks, reliability scorecards, dashboards.  
3. Practice articulating how you handle conflict, feedback, and prioritisation.  
4. Prepare metrics illustrating team health and delivery impact.

## Mock Interview Structure (45 minutes)
| Segment | Objective | Repo Sections |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 5 min | Leadership philosophy | Root README |
| 15 min | Team coaching and culture | Coaching modules |
| 15 min | Delivery and reliability example | Reliability metrics, retrospectives |
| 5 min | Stakeholder alignment story | Communication templates |
| 5 min | Candidate questions | Below |

## Sample Prompts
- “Describe how you handled a conflict between speed and reliability.”  
- “How do you mentor engineers into leadership roles?”  
- “Walk me through a process improvement you introduced and its impact.”

## Behavioral Prompts
- Difficult conversations with underperforming engineers  
- Reacting to shifting priorities from product leadership  
- Sustaining psychological safety during high-pressure periods

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer
- “How do you measure success for engineering leaders here?”  
- “What support structures exist for leadership development?”  
- “How are incidents and retros handled across teams?”

## Next Steps
Document your leadership playbook, attach supportive artefacts, and review after each new team milestone or retro.***
